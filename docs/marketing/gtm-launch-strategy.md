## 1. The one-paragraph strategy

Good Local wins region one with a **two-sided, density-first launch**: founder-led sales close
10 to 25 founding businesses in a tight Narrowsburg cluster before June 20 (the Mel gate), then
the register QR becomes the patron distribution channel from June 30. Marketing's job is not mass
reach. It is (a) making the $79 founding decision feel obvious and time-boxed for ~150 owners,
(b) making "add the passport" a 10-second yes for weekenders already standing at the register, and
(c) building the organic content engine (blog, podcast, town pages) that compounds into local
search ownership by Labor Day. The launch film is the emotional spine all three reuse.

**Elite-practice grounding** (from research): tight ICP beats broad reach; the first 10 customers
come from founder-led sales, not funnels; buyers self-educate through ~80% of the journey before
talking to anyone (the landing + blog must carry the pitch alone); community social capital creates
switching costs no feature can; story-led launch videos convert up to ~80% better than
feature-led; and in 2026 **authenticity beats studio polish** — real places and real people
outperform synthetic perfection. That last point is load-bearing for a trust-first local brand.

## 2. Positioning (the words everything else reuses)

- **For business owners** (the paying customer): "Your own loyalty program, working the day you
  sign up. Stamps per visit, perks you design, a dashboard that shows who comes back. $79 a month,
  no contract. And every neighbor who joins makes your program worth more: one regional passport
  turns their customers into your prospects."
- **For patrons** (the network): "One free passport for the whole river. Earn a stamp every visit
  at the places you love. Perks from the owners themselves. Recommendations come from one source
  only: where verified regulars actually return. Never paid placement."
- **Competitive frame:** paper punch cards (no data), Square/Toast loyalty (POS-locked, siloed),
  Loopy ($25 to 95, stamps without the dashboard, the network, or the region). We are not selling
  stamps. We are selling the dashboard plus the region's gravity.
- **Trust posture as PR hook:** "ranked by verified return visits, never paid placement" is the
  quotable line for every press pitch. No competitor can say it.

## 3. The two-sided funnel and channel plan

### B side — founding businesses (now → June 20, founder-led)
1. **Mel walks Main Street.** In-person, relationship-first, Narrowsburg → Barryville/Eldred →
   Callicoon. Marketing arms the walk: a one-page leave-behind (the $79 math vs one lost regular),
   the business cut of the launch film on a phone, and a live demo of check-in → stamp → dashboard.
2. **Founding scarcity is real, not manufactured:** founding rate lock, launch-marketing placement,
   winter tier. Deadline is the launch date itself.
3. **Same-day setup is the close.** Sign up at the table, print the QR kit before leaving.

### C side — patrons (June 30 →)
1. **The register QR is the channel.** Every participating counter is a storefront for the
   passport. Patron acquisition cost approaches zero once the B side is dense.
2. **Local Facebook groups + weekender Instagram** carry the launch film cutdowns. Link previews
   matter here, which makes the OG/meta work in the SEO backlog a marketing dependency, not a
   nice-to-have.
3. **Earned local press:** River Reporter, Sullivan County Democrat, regional newsletters and
   podcasts. Pitch the story as "river towns market themselves together," anchored by the film.
4. **SCVA co-marketing** (letter of support track per the PR/FAQ; no money asked, no dependency).

### Owned content engine (the organic compounding loop)
- **Blog as local SEO engine:** town guides ("A regular's day in Narrowsburg"), founding business
  spotlights (each one a page that the owner shares), seasonal river content. Every participating
  business becomes an indexable page over time.
- **Podcast:** owner interviews. The guest shares the episode; their audience meets the passport.
- **Cadence:** one post + one episode per week through Labor Day. Thin pages are the current
  weakest link (HANDOFF next step 2); the launch needs 3 posts + 1 episode live by June 27.

## 4. The launch film: creative strategy

**Format (research-backed):** one **75 second hero film** (landing page, press, YouTube) plus
cutdowns: **30s patron vertical** (IG/TikTok/FB), **45s owner cut** (the $79 pitch, dashboard
forward), and **6 × 15s teasers**. Structure is Hook → Problem → Solution → Benefit → one CTA.
30 to 90 seconds is the highest-performing band; never bury the reveal past second 20.

**Creative concept — "Become a regular":** the film follows one weekender couple across a season.
First visit: strangers floating past storefronts. Stamp by stamp, the river corridor opens up:
the café knows their order, the bookstore holds the shelf, the fifth stamp unlocks the regular's
pour. Closing line over the seal mark:

> Copy block (house style compliant):
> "Every stamp is a place that knows you now. Good Local. The Upper Delaware Passport.
> Free in your wallet at any participating register."

**The authenticity guardrail (binding):** Good Local's entire trust posture is verified reality.
Therefore: **real footage for real claims, AI for atmosphere.** Real smartphone/mirrorless footage
of actual founding businesses, real owners, Mel, the founders. AI generation is used for what a
$0 budget cannot shoot: golden-hour river aerials, drifting canoe wides, seasonal time-lapse
moods, the wallet-pass macro animations. Never AI-generate a fake storefront, a fake customer,
a fake testimonial, or imply traction that does not exist (pattern 134: no fake stats, ever).

## 5. AI tool recommendation (the research verdict)

**Field status, June 2026:** Sora 2 is **discontinued** (OpenAI killed the app April 26, 2026;
API dies September 24, 2026) — excluded. The community consensus across Reddit/X benchmarks:

| Tool | Strength | Cost signal | Verdict for us |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Veo 3.1** | Best all-round quality, prompt adherence, native synchronized audio, 4K, landscape + portrait | Gemini AI Pro tier (~$20/mo) covers low volume; per-second via API/Flow | **Primary: hero film scenes** |
| **Runway Gen-4.5** | Most creative control (camera moves, motion brush, references, built-in editor), character consistency | ~$12 to 95/mo tiers | **Secondary: shot fixes + the edit layer** |
| **Kling 3.0 Omni** | Cheapest premium (~$0.10/s), multi-shot consistency, lip sync | Lowest cost at volume | Volume cutdowns / teaser variants |
| **Seedance 2.0** | Hyper-realism, currently winning community head-to-heads, UGC-style ads | Very cheap | Social UGC-style variants |
| **HeyGen / Synthesia** | Avatar + Cinematic Avatar API (automated launch videos) | $24+/mo | **Not for us** — avatar spokespeople contradict the authenticity posture; real humans on camera instead |

**Recommendation: Veo 3.1 as the primary engine** (best quality + native audio + both aspect
ratios from one model), **Runway Gen-4.5 as the control/edit layer** when a shot needs camera
direction or a fix, and **Kling or Seedance for the 15s social variants** where volume matters
more than fidelity. Expected total tool spend for the entire launch package: **under $150**,
versus $500 to $5,000 per finished minute for traditional production.

The single strongest insight from the research, however: *"The advantage is no longer the tool.
The advantage is creative strategy, storytelling, hook writing."* The tool list above is the
commodity layer; sections 2 to 4 of this document are the moat.

**Workflow (community-proven):** image-to-video pipeline. (1) Shoot real footage day with founding
businesses. (2) Generate stills of the AI scenes first (frame, light, composition approved as
images). (3) Animate approved stills in Veo 3.1 with structured prompts (below). (4) Assemble in
Runway's editor or CapCut: real footage carries people and places, AI carries atmosphere.
(5) Export 16:9 hero + 9:16 cutdowns.

### Ready-to-use Veo 3.1 prompt pattern (structured, per research)

```
Scene: slow aerial drift over a forested river valley at golden hour, a canoe
  trailing a soft wake on calm water, small river town with white church steeple
  and clapboard storefronts on the far bank
Style: warm documentary realism, Kodak-toned, gentle film grain, no people in frame
Camera: high wide, slow push forward, 6 seconds, subtle parallax
Audio: river ambience, distant wood thrush, no music
Constraints: photoreal, no text, no signage with readable words, no watermarks
Aspect: 16:9, 4K
```

Repeat the pattern per storyboard scene (canoe wide, main street dusk, rain on a café window,
autumn turn for the winter-tier beat). Keep readable signage out of AI shots so no real business
is misrepresented; real signage comes from the real footage day.

## 6. Measurement (ties to existing gates, adds marketing leading indicators)

Season gates (D-007) stay the source of truth: breadth ≥40%, depth repeat ≥15%, steered ≥25%,
250 to 500 actives by Labor Day, ≥15 paying founding businesses by June 20 (pre-gate ≥10).
Marketing adds leading indicators, all already instrumentable: passport adds per founding business
per week (target 30 to 50 per business per the PR/FAQ), region-interest leads per week (the
Regions admin tab), blog/podcast traffic once analytics lands (audit dependency), film
view-through and click-through on the landing hero.
