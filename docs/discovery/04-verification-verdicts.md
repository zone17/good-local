## The five gaps (completeness critic — nobody had covered these)

1. **Verified-regulars ranking has a cold-start problem — and the P1 revision just made it the headline value.** A ranking computed on day-one data is empty; the dashboard owners are asked to pay for is also empty during the exact window WTP is tested. **Fix (free):** season-one cold-start policy — no computed ranking; ship hand-curated "founding regulars' picks" + a visible per-business "X verified regulars this season" counter. Sell the dashboard as value that *accrues over the season*.
2. **Perk economics / cannibalization.** 30–50% of promo revenue is typically cannibalized; milestone perks by construction reward the customers who'd come anyway, on peak days. **Fix (free):** perk-design constraint in the founding agreement — low-marginal-cost, visit-shaped, off-peak, status-good perks ("regular's table," early access) — plus a Mel Test question: "what could you give a 5th-visit regular that costs you almost nothing but feels special?"
3. **Mel is a single point of failure** — named in the POV, gating the launch, with only one modeled failure mode (<10 signatures). Unmodeled: businesses converting to *Mel* rather than to the product. **Fix (free):** September attribution question ("if Mel weren't involved, would you still renew?") + an explicit season-one deliverable: recruit one second local relationship-holder.
4. **Check-in integrity was never specified.** Every metric, gate, ranking, and perk trusts a check-in; an open static QR lets owners self-inflate rankings and lets the Aug 15 gate be *passed by fraud*. **Fix (near-free):** v1 trust model — server-rotating QR codes + per-device per-business daily rate limits; fold an anti-gaming sentence into the A10 legal scope.
5. **The runway question nobody asked.** Stacked verdicts = ~11 months (June 2026 → May 2027) at essentially zero revenue, DMO money pushed to 2027, paid placement ruled out, market sized down. **Fix (free but mandatory):** new assumption **A12 — "We can fund ~11 months to first revenue at planned burn"** with an honest number; relabel the September founding deposits as a cash *signal*, not funding.

**Evidence-modality gap:** every artifact is desk research — zero primary evidence from any actual Upper Delaware weekender or owner. The already-budgeted touchpoints (the $250 landing test, the A5 field interviews, the Mel Test) get three added Mom-Test questions (perk value, Mel attribution, empty-ranking reaction) so the one primary-evidence channel does triple duty before launch.

## Post-verification founder decisions (June 6, 2026 — recorded for provenance)

After these verdicts were delivered, two founder decisions amended the verified bet — both recorded with the evidence on each side in PR/FAQ v2:

1. **$79/mo from day one** (overrides the free-founding-season element of P6's revision; keeps its seasonality core as a November 1 retention gate). Effect: the cash WTP signal moves from September to the June founding push; the A2 gate becomes paid signups; the A2/A3 cards were updated accordingly.
2. **Standalone-first architecture**: each business's own rewards program (stamps, perks, dashboard, QR kit) is fully functional on day one with zero other participants; the passport/network layer compounds on top. This is what makes day-one pricing coherent — nobody pays for an empty network — and it directly mitigates the P1 price-defense and G1 cold-start findings.

## Net effect on the bet

The refined bet's **direction is confirmed** — passport spine, weekender beachhead, points demotion, seasonality-aware billing all stand on verified evidence. What changed: the gates are now anchored to real comparables instead of aspiration (250–500 actives, not 2,000), the kill condition can no longer be passed by collection-toy behavior or fraud, the DMO is a 2027 track not a 2026 savior, the first willingness-to-pay signal is **cash in September** instead of intent, distribution is an open A/B (wallet pass favored) instead of an assumption, the date is gated on a demoable build, and the company now has to write down who funds 11 months.
