Discovery · 04 Adversarial Verification · June 6, 2026

Seven decisions attacked. Seven held — all revised.

An adversarial workflow of 15 agents — one refuter per refinement decision (web evidence required, instructed to break it), one neutral judge per point (independently re-verified the refuters' sources), and one completeness critic over the whole bet. 191 tool calls.

7/7 held 0 overturned 1 factual error caught 5 gaps closed
Outcome

7/7 decisions held — none overturned — all 7 weakened with specific, source-verified revisions. One factual error found and corrected. Five gaps identified that no prior artifact covered. The method: one refuter per refinement decision, one neutral judge re-verifying each refuter's sources, and one completeness critic over the whole bet — 15 agents, 191 tool calls.

The Seven Verdicts

P1–P7 — every decision weakened, none broken.

Each verdict pairs what the attack proved (verified against web evidence) with the required revision the founders accepted.

P1 · Passport mechanic Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
DMO trail passports reliably produce one-and-done collection, not repeat visits (Pigeon Forge 0.29 redemptions/user; Detroit 0.31; Omaha 0.37; Nebraska Beef ~1.3 check-ins/user). Loopy Loyalty sells a multi-merchant community stamp card at $25–69 — the stamp loop is commoditized. Business-funded milestone perks shrink, not kill, the fairness problem.
Required revision
Passport spine stays, but: depth mechanic = same-business repeat visits (stamps per visit, not per location); split breadth vs depth gates; price defense = verified-regulars ranking + owner dashboard (Loopy has neither), passport is the acquisition hook; explicit perk fairness governance.

P2 · New gates Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
"2,000 patrons with 2+ check-ins" implies 120–265 signups/merchant — 3–15× the comparable norm (Knoxville ~17/merchant; Nebraska ~36/merchant). The 40%/25% rate gates and Aug-15/n≥200 kill are sound.
Required revision
Volume gate re-anchored: 30–50 signups per founding business → ~250–500 active patrons by Labor Day; 2,000 becomes a year-2 stretch goal. Volume kill at <300 active, not <2,000. 25% gate scoped to active (2+ check-in) patrons.

P3 · Weekender beachhead Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
Beachhead logic survives, but the cluster realistically reaches low-single-thousands of households; the funnel math can land season-one N below the n≥200 gate-reading floor. Excluding QR-averse locals concedes the only patron base physically present Oct–April.
Required revision
Sample-adequacy pre-gate: <500 installs by July 31 → A1 result is INCONCLUSIVE (extend/widen), not pass/fail. Core loop = season-cumulative passport progress (no time-decay); streaks demoted to optional layer. Add a non-QR enrollment path (staff-entered phone / wallet pass) so locals can carry winter.

P4 · DMO channel Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
Factual error caught: there is no "June 2026 Catalyst Grant round." 2026 rounds closed Dec 16, 2025 and April 14, 2026; June is disbursement to already-selected winners. Next application ~Nov 2026, awarded ~Jan 2027 — after the kill gate. SCVA is also an incumbent with overlapping product (GO! app, free listings since May 2025, sells Key Data/Datafy merchant analytics).
Required revision
A4 demoted from pre-launch de-risker to 2027 viability track. Near-term goal: letter of support + co-marketing from one meeting; money contingent on season-one data feeding a 2027 application. Position as complement (verified-return-visit data SCVA lacks). Founder time reallocated to A2 + A1.

P5 · Points demotion Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
The justification over-read Fivestars/Capitola: Odio's verbatim hypothesis tested cross-merchant deal advertising, not shared-currency redemption. The decision still stands on directly on-point evidence: Plenti's 1–2-partner redemption concentration, Air Miles collapse, points liability, and the scale dependency (Payback needs ~35M members/700 partners to reach 6.35 partners/customer — unreachable at 15–75 merchants).
Required revision
Demotion stays. Re-label Capitola as Medium-relevance (deal advertising, not redemption); strike "single best natural experiment." Re-anchor on Plenti/Air Miles/scale. Define the Phase-2 gate concretely: a network-funded-credit path (inKind-style, liability held by a DMO/grant funder).

P6 · Billing seasonality Weakened

What the attack proved (verified)
Internal contradiction caught: the A3 PASS gate graded on unpaid renewal-intent signatures — exactly the stated-intent evidence the bet's own Mom Test rule calls weak. And SCVA's May 2025 free-membership pivot anchors merchant WTP toward $0 (missed by discovery).
Required revision
Seasonality core stays (never first-invoice in October). A3 converts from intent to cash: PASS = card-on-file pre-auth for May 2027 or a refundable founding deposit ($99–199, credited), collected in September. Hibernate tier reframed as a year-round "winter value" tier. Explicit anti-$0-anchor positioning: sell retention ROI + verified-regulars data, not listings (SCVA gives those away).

P7 · Unchanged items Weakened (2 of 7 items)

What the attack proved (verified)
Distribution: "validated PWA-first" contradicted our own evidence (claim #13: <5% install single-business apps; insight #5 named wallet passes a twin workaround). Apple Wallet passes: zero install, ~10 geofenced locations, lock-screen surfacing — directly serves the A1 loop iOS PWA push can't reach without Add-to-Home-Screen. Date: 24 days is below corroborated MVP base rates (8–16 weeks) with no team named; the brief's own pre-gate already says "the date moves, scope does not." Price / no-paid-placement / POS moat / legal: hold.
Required revision
Distribution → OPEN: wallet-pass-first is the leading issuance candidate; thin web app for directory + dashboard. Resolve via free A/B inside the $250 landing test ("Add to Wallet" vs "Add to Home Screen"). Date → target gated on build readiness: vertical slice (check-in → wallet pass) demoable by ~June 18, else slide to July 15 (still ~7 peak weeks). No-paid-placement relabeled "validated as trust posture; untested as revenue ceiling."
Completeness Critic

The five gaps nobody had covered.

Each carries an interaction risk and a cheapest fix — four free, one near-free, one free-but-mandatory.

1

Verified-regulars ranking has a cold-start problem

And the P1 revision just made it the headline value. A ranking computed on day-one data is empty; the dashboard owners are asked to pay for is also empty during the exact window WTP is tested.

Cheapest fix
(free) Season-one cold-start policy — no computed ranking; ship hand-curated "founding regulars' picks" + a visible per-business "X verified regulars this season" counter. Sell the dashboard as value that accrues over the season.
2

Perk economics / cannibalization

30–50% of promo revenue is typically cannibalized; milestone perks by construction reward the customers who'd come anyway, on peak days.

Cheapest fix
(free) Perk-design constraint in the founding agreement — low-marginal-cost, visit-shaped, off-peak, status-good perks ("regular's table," early access) — plus a Mel Test question: "what could you give a 5th-visit regular that costs you almost nothing but feels special?"
3

Mel is a single point of failure

Named in the POV, gating the launch, with only one modeled failure mode (<10 signatures). Unmodeled: businesses converting to Mel rather than to the product.

Cheapest fix
(free) September attribution question ("if Mel weren't involved, would you still renew?") + an explicit season-one deliverable: recruit one second local relationship-holder.
4

Check-in integrity was never specified

Every metric, gate, ranking, and perk trusts a check-in; an open static QR lets owners self-inflate rankings and lets the Aug 15 gate be passed by fraud.

Cheapest fix
(near-free) v1 trust model — server-rotating QR codes + per-device per-business daily rate limits; fold an anti-gaming sentence into the A10 legal scope.
5

The runway question nobody asked

Stacked verdicts = ~11 months (June 2026 → May 2027) at essentially zero revenue, DMO money pushed to 2027, paid placement ruled out, market sized down.

Cheapest fix
(free but mandatory) New assumption A12 — "We can fund ~11 months to first revenue at planned burn" with an honest number; relabel the September founding deposits as a cash signal, not funding.

Evidence-modality gap: every artifact is desk research — zero primary evidence from any actual Upper Delaware weekender or owner. The already-budgeted touchpoints (the $250 landing test, the A5 field interviews, the Mel Test) get three added Mom-Test questions (perk value, Mel attribution, empty-ranking reaction) so the one primary-evidence channel does triple duty before launch.

Post-Verification Founder Decisions · Provenance

After these verdicts were delivered, two founder decisions amended the verified bet — both recorded with the evidence on each side in PR/FAQ v2:

  1. $79/mo from day one (overrides the free-founding-season element of P6's revision; keeps its seasonality core as a November 1 retention gate). Effect: the cash WTP signal moves from September to the June founding push; the A2 gate becomes paid signups; the A2/A3 cards were updated accordingly.
  2. Standalone-first architecture: each business's own rewards program (stamps, perks, dashboard, QR kit) is fully functional on day one with zero other participants; the passport/network layer compounds on top. This is what makes day-one pricing coherent — nobody pays for an empty network — and it directly mitigates the P1 price-defense and G1 cold-start findings.
Net Effect

The direction is confirmed — the gates are now real.

The refined bet's direction is confirmed — passport spine, weekender beachhead, points demotion, seasonality-aware billing all stand on verified evidence. What changed: the gates are now anchored to real comparables instead of aspiration (250–500 actives, not 2,000), the kill condition can no longer be passed by collection-toy behavior or fraud, the DMO is a 2027 track not a 2026 savior, the first willingness-to-pay signal is cash in September instead of intent, distribution is an open A/B (wallet pass favored) instead of an assumption, the date is gated on a demoable build, and the company now has to write down who funds 11 months.
