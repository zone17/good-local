## The sharp problem

```
WHO:          Owner-operated independent businesses in the Upper Delaware region
              (~150–250 of them; cafés, restaurants, inns, outfitters, shops,
              galleries) — and the NYC weekenders who want to belong there.
STRUGGLE:     When the season is running, owners are trying to turn one-time
              and occasional patrons into regulars — but they have no way to
              see, reach, or reward repeat customers without becoming
              marketers, and every loyalty tool sold to them was built for a
              single business in a big market.
              When weekenders are upstate, they are trying to find the places
              locals actually return to — and to be recognized somewhere as a
              regular — but discovery runs on anonymous star ratings and
              accidental word of mouth.
CONTEXT:      Continuously May–October (377k river visits/yr, $969M county
              visitor spend, ~37–40% second-home housing share); collapses
              October–April.
ALTERNATIVES: Paper punch cards (no data, single-business), Square/Toast
              loyalty (siloed, $49–$185/mo, POS-locked), Yelp/Google
              (anonymous, pay-to-promote), nothing cross-business — the
              region has no loyalty or trusted-discovery layer at all.
WHY IT MATTERS: Retention is worth 5–25× acquisition; >1/3 of operators are
              dissatisfied with loyalty tools; and the discovery-trust seat
              has been empty since Foursquare exited consumers (Dec 2024).
EVIDENCE:     Census/NPS/Tourism Economics (High); complaint-record customer
              voice (High); weekender behavior first-hand (NONE — riskiest
              assumption, test A1). Overall confidence: Medium-High on the
              problem, Low on the mechanism until A1 runs.
```

*Passes the multiple-solutions test: a passport, a points coalition, a gift-card network, and white-label punch cards could all address it.*

## What changes in the PR/FAQ (the refine list)

*(v2 — each point revised per the adversarial verification, `04-verification-verdicts.md`.)*

1. **Mechanism:** cross-business points redemption → **regional passport with two loops**: depth (same-business repeat visits — stamps per visit, never one-per-location; the owner-renewal driver) and breadth (season-cumulative progress, no time-decay; streaks optional). Perks: business-funded under explicit fairness governance + a perk-design constraint (low-marginal-cost, visit-shaped, off-peak, status goods — never peak-day discounts). The paid value is the **verified-regulars ranking + owner dashboard** (Loopy at $25–69 has neither); cold-start policy: curated founding picks + regulars counters, no computed ranking at launch. Honest evidence note: the fairness problem is *shrunk*, not killed; Fivestars/Capitola indicted cross-merchant deal advertising, not shared-currency redemption — the points demotion rests on Plenti/Air Miles/scale-dependency instead.
2. **Gate metrics (re-anchored to comparables):** breadth ≥40% 2nd-business (target); **depth ≥15% same-business repeat + median ≥2 check-ins/active** (the collection-toy detectors); steered first visits ≥25% of *active* patrons; volume 30–50 signups/business → **250–500 actives by Labor Day** (2,000 = year-2 stretch). Kill reads require n≥200 and ≥500 installs by July 31 (else inconclusive); volume kill <300.
3. **Beachhead patron:** weekender-first (sized honestly: low-single-thousands of reachable households) — **with a non-QR enrollment path** (staff-entered phone / wallet pass) so year-round locals can carry the October–April shoulder.
4. **Channel:** SCVA/DMO is a **2027 track**, not a season-one de-risker — the "June 2026 grant round" was a factual error (2026 rounds closed Dec 16, 2025 and April 14, 2026). Near-term: one meeting for a letter of support + co-marketing, positioned as *complementing* SCVA (verified-return-visit data they lack) — SCVA is an incumbent with an overlapping free product, not a neutral sponsor. Founder time goes to the Mel Test and the build.
5. **Billing — superseded by founder decision (June 6, 2026):** **$79/mo from day one** — no free season (overriding the cash-hardened-free-season recommendation; both sides of the evidence recorded in PR/FAQ v2). Coherent because of the second founder decision, **standalone-first architecture**: day one, each business's own rewards program (stamps, perks, promotions, dashboard, QR kit) works with zero other participants — the network compounds on top, so nobody pays for an empty network. $49/mo winter-value tier Nov–Apr; founding-rate lock. Effects: the cash WTP signal moves from September to the June founding push (A2 and A3 partially merge); the June 20 gate gets harder and the date (not scope) absorbs a miss; the seasonality core survives as a **November 1 retention gate (≥60% still paying or on winter tier)** instead of a first-invoice cliff. Anti-$0-anchor positioning vs SCVA's free membership unchanged.
6. **Numbers:** region population ~150–180k (was 100–150k); river visits 377k/yr and rising; business TAM in region 150–250.
7. **Split verdicts (was "unchanged and validated"):** **HOLD** — $79 price, no paid placement (as *trust posture*; untested as revenue ceiling), 3–5yr POS-incumbent moat, NY+PA legal review (now including the check-in anti-gaming requirement). **OPEN** — distribution: wallet-pass-first is the leading issuance candidate (zero install, geofenced lock-screen surfacing) with a thin web app for directory/dashboard; resolve via a free "Add to Wallet" vs "Add to Home Screen" A/B inside the $250 landing test. **GATED** — June 30 is a *target*, conditional on a demoable vertical slice by ~June 18; fallback July 15 (still ~7 peak weeks).
8. **New (from the gap analysis):** **A12 — runway**: ~11 months to first revenue (June 2026 → May 2027) must be funded at planned burn; write the number down. **Check-in trust model** in v1 scope (rotating QR + rate limits). **Mel de-risking**: September attribution question ("would you renew if Mel weren't involved?") + recruit a second local champion as a season-one deliverable. The $250 landing test and A5 field interviews each carry three added Mom-Test questions (perk value, Mel attribution, empty-ranking reaction) — the only primary-evidence touchpoints before launch must do triple duty.
