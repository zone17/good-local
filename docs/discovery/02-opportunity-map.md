# Good Local — Discovery Artifact 2: Opportunity Map

> **Date:** June 6, 2026 · **Method:** Opportunity-solution tree (Torres) + ODI opportunity scores. Scores are desk-research estimates (Importance/Satisfaction 1–10), flagged as such — they prioritize, they don't prove.

## Desired outcome (root)

**Maximize repeat and cross-business visits to independent Upper Delaware businesses per patron per season** — measured as verified visits attributable to the network. (An outcome, not a feature. The PR/FAQ's business gate — owners renewing at $79/mo — follows from this or doesn't happen.)

## Opportunity space (problems, customer-voiced — each admits multiple solutions)

```
OUTCOME: more repeat + cross-business visits per patron per season
│
├── O1 OWNER: "I need my regulars back more often without becoming a marketer"
│     ├── O1a "I have no way to know who my repeat customers are" (data void)
│     └── O1b "Loyalty tools burned me — contracts, opaque ROI, staff hassle"
│
├── O2 OWNER: "People pass my door all season and never come in" (visitor leakage)
│
├── O3 WEEKENDER: "I want to find the spots locals actually go — and become
│     a regular here, not just a visitor" (discovery + belonging)
│
├── O4 VISITOR: "I'm here for the day; I don't know what else is worth a stop"
│
├── O5 LOCAL: "I support these places year-round and nothing recognizes it"
│
└── O6 REGION (DMO/chamber): "We market the destination but can't measure or
      steer where visitors actually spend" (the institutional job)
```

**Reading:** O1 (15) and O3 (14) are the under-served core — and they are two sides of one transaction: *the owner wants the weekender back; the weekender wants to be a regular.* O6 (11) is not the sharpest problem but is the **channel** that de-risks everything (free merchant enrollment, winter-proof revenue). O4/O5 are over-served or low-importance — don't build for them first; let them ride along.

## Solution space (3+ competing solutions for the target opportunity O1+O3)

| # | Solution | What it is | Evidence for | Evidence against |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S1 — PR/FAQ's bet: shared points network** (check-in → points → cross-redeem) | One currency, earn anywhere, redeem anywhere | Coalition spillover paper (incumbents gain when network grows); Joe Coffee works in-vertical | **Plenti redemption concentration + anchor-exit death; Air Miles concentration collapse; points liability/fairness; scale dependency (Payback: ~35M members/700 partners → only 6.35 partners/customer); no benchmark supports 15% cross-redemption.** *(Corrected per `04`/P5: the Fivestars Capitola test indicted cross-merchant deal advertising, not shared currency — Medium relevance here, not the headline evidence.)* |
| **S2 — Regional passport, two loops** (check-in → stamps **per visit, never one-per-location** → business-funded perks; no inter-business point transfers) | DEPTH: same-business repeat visits — the owner-renewal driver. BREADTH: season-cumulative regional progress (no time-decay; streaks optional). *(Reframed per `04`/P1+P3: DMO trail passports produce one-and-done collection — Pigeon Forge 0.29 redemptions/user — so the trail is the hook, never the engine.)* | Bandwango's category proves the breadth behavior; stamp-per-visit supported (Loopy docs); zero cross-business currency liability; simplest ledger | **Breadth ≠ depth — the collection-toy failure mode (detect via median check-ins & same-business repeat gates)**; stamp loop commoditized at $25–69 (Loopy Unified Community Card) — paid value must be the ranking + dashboard; perk fairness *shrunk*, not killed — needs governance |
| **S3 — Community gift card + discovery app** (Yiftee-style value card; app adds trusted rankings) | Dollar-denominated, spend anywhere in network | Yiftee survives where points coalitions died; 30% overspend vs face value; no fairness dispute (real $ settle) | Needs a funder (chamber/DMO/ARPA) for bonus mechanics; weaker gamification; payments infra in v1 (June 30 infeasible) |
| **S4 — Single-business white-label loyalty sold town-wide** | Loopy-style digital punch cards, shared brand | Proven mechanic; cheapest | Abandons the network thesis entirely; loses to Loopy at $25/mo on price |
| **S5 — DMO-sponsored hybrid: passport spine + perk marketplace** (S2 mechanics, SCVA as anchor sponsor, owner dashboard from O1a) | Merchants free or near-free season one; Good Local sells the region, not 75 subscriptions | Bandwango model proof; SCVA $300k program exists; kills the Belly density problem and the winter churn problem at once | **2026 grant rounds already closed (Dec 2025 / April 2026 — `04`/P4); next application ~Nov 2026, awarded ~Jan 2027 — a 2027 track, not a season-one de-risker**; program funds events/cultural orgs, not tech; SCVA is an incumbent with an overlapping free product (GO! app, free listings) and a $0 anchor on merchant WTP; single-buyer concentration |

**Comparison verdict:** S1 — the PR/FAQ's exact mechanic — is the *weakest-evidenced* of the credible options on its load-bearing claim (cross-redemption). **S2's passport spine carries the same product promise (check in, build streaks, unlock perks, discover via verified regulars) with none of the cross-business liability** that killed coalitions, and it is buildable by June 30. S5 is S2's go-to-market amplifier, not a different product. S3 is the right *Phase 2* shape for money mechanics (value-denominated beats points when payments arrive). S4 is the fallback if the network thesis dies.

## Recommended bet (feeds the brief)

**Pursue O1+O3 with S2 (two-loop passport) now and S5 as the 2027 channel: a weekender-first regional passport — depth loop for owners, breadth loop for discovery — with business-funded perks under governance, verified-regulars ranking + owner dashboard as the paid value, sold at $79/mo to founding businesses; SCVA engaged now for a letter of support and positioned as a complement, with money following season-one data into the ~Nov 2026 grant cycle.** Points-as-shared-currency (S1) is demoted to a Phase-2 experiment gated on a network-funded-credit design with a real liability holder (inKind-style; links to S3/S5). This keeps every promise in the press release that evidence supports, and drops the one mechanic the evidence indicts.

**Is the PR/FAQ chasing the sharpest opportunity?** Directionally yes (O1+O3 are the top scores) — but it bet on the wrong *mechanism* (cross-redemption points) and mis-timed the channel (O6/DMO is a 2027 amplifier, not a season-one de-risker). Verdict: **refine, not pivot** — adversarially verified in `04-verification-verdicts.md` (7/7 held with revisions).
