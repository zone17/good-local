# Good Local — Earnings Model

> **Date:** June 6, 2026 · **Pricing basis:** founder decision — **$79/mo per business from day one** (no free season), optional $49/mo winter tier Nov–Apr, founding-rate lock. Free for patrons. All figures are pre-launch estimates anchored to the discovery's verified comparables — they are planning numbers, not forecasts. Update after the June 20 Mel gate and again at Labor Day.

## 1. Revenue lines (what can ever make money)

| Line | Status | Mechanics | Scale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Business SaaS** | **Live day one** | $79/mo in season; $49/mo winter tier Nov–Apr; founding-rate lock; no contract | The engine — ~90% gross margin ($79 vs ~$8/mo marginal cost) |
| **Payments margin** | Phase 2 (post-gates) | In-app payments at ~0.4% net after processing; at 20% share of a $150k-GPV business ≈ **~$120/business/yr** | Meaningful only at hundreds of businesses |
| **DMO / tourism sponsorship** | 2027 track | SCVA grant cycle (apply ~Nov 2026, awarded ~Jan 2027) or sponsorship line; Bandwango-pattern | $15–50k/region/yr where it lands; never load-bearing |
| **Shared-currency / network credit** | Phase-2 experiment, gated | Only with a real liability holder (inKind-style credit pool) | Unmodeled until the gate passes |
| ~~Paid placement~~ | **Excluded permanently** | Trust posture — flagged honestly as an untested revenue ceiling | $0 forever by choice |

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** (Mel gate scraped: 10–12) | ~11 | ~$3.5k | ~$2.4k | **~$6k** |

## 5. Year three — regions multiply (only if the playbook travels)

The gating question is not demand — it's **whether region #2 has a Mel** (the second-champion deliverable exists precisely for this). Region economics: ~$60–90k launch cost → ~$55–70k ARR at maturity → **12–15 month payback**, partially offset by day-one billing.

| Scenario | Regions live | Businesses | **ARR run-rate exiting year 3** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative (playbook doesn't travel) | 1 | ~75 | **~$56k** |
| Base | 2–3 | ~170 | **~$130k SaaS + ~$20k payments + ~$30k DMO ≈ $180k** |
| Stretch | 4–5 | ~300 | **~$225k SaaS + ~$36k payments + ~$60k DMO ≈ $320k** |

**Honest framing (unchanged from discovery):** venture-scale only if the region playbook compounds; a sound bootstrap/lifestyle business at 5–15 regions if it doesn't. Charging from day one fits the bootstrap path especially well — every region is cash-generative within its first season.

## 7. Costs against it (A12 — the runway answer)

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 — check-in MVP, QR kits, launch (this season) | ~$50–100k |
| Year-one revenue against it | ~$6–19k (covers 10–25%) |
| Phase 2 — payments, hardened ledger, region playbook (if gates pass) | ~$800k–1.2M over 12 months |
| Region launch (each, after #1) | ~$60–90k, 12–15 mo payback |

**The A12 line, plainly:** day-one billing is a real signal and a real offset, but it does not fund the company. The gap between ~$15k of year-one revenue and ~$50–100k of Phase-1 cost — and the Phase-2 raise behind it — must have a named funding source before code is written.

## 8. Sensitivities (what moves these numbers most, in order)

1. **The June 20 Mel gate at $79** — the whole model's first branch. <10 paying → date moves and every table above shifts right by weeks.
2. **The depth gates (Aug 15)** — if patrons don't become regulars, year-2+ renewal collapses regardless of season-one billing; the surviving business is per-business SaaS in a price war with Loopy at $25–69.
3. **November retention (≥60% gate)** — winter is the recurring-revenue killer in a seasonal region; the $49 tier exists to flatten it.
4. **A second champion** — determines whether year 3 has one row or three.
5. SMB SaaS churn gravity (3–7%/mo benchmark) vs founding-rate lock-in.
